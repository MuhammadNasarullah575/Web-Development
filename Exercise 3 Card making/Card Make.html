Faisal Mosque

Faisal Mosque is the largest mosque in Pakistan and a famous landmark of the capital city, Islamabad.It is located at the foothills of the Margalla Hills, which makes its scenery very beautiful.The mosque was designed by the Turkish architect Vedat Dalokay.Its design is unique because it looks like a desert tent instead of a traditional dome-shaped mosque.Construction of the mosque started in 1976 and was completed in 1986.It was funded by King Faisal bin Abdulaziz Al Saud of Saudi Arabia.Thousands of people can offer prayers in the mosque at the same time.Today, Faisal Mosque is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Pakistan.
